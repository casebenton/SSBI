Leadership

Student Leaders

Michael Chen ('18)

President

Michael is an undergraduate at Stanford who loves working at the intersection of neuroscience, medicine, and public service. Having suffered a minor brain injury himself as a child, Michael became interested in supporting individuals with brain injury and raising public awareness about brain injury after arriving at Stanford. Michael worked with medical students Alissa Totman and Jackie Konopka to establish Stanford Synapse as an official student organization at Stanford. Since then, he has had the privilege of leading a team of passionate Stanford students to expand the group’s reach and impact on the brain injury community. After witnessing firsthand the importance of social support in the recovery process, Michael has organized initiatives and events to bring individuals with brain injury and Stanford students together in a safe and supportive environment. More recently, Michael has been involved in the founding of Synapse National, which is extending the model of the student-run support group to create a network of brain injury support groups at colleges across the country. After graduation, Michael plans to research and treat disorders of the brain as a physician scientist.

Sophia Zhang ('18)

Vice President of Events

Sophie studies Biology, with a concentration in Neurobiology, and Art Practice at Stanford. In her second year, Sophie assisted with the launch and development of Stanford Synapse as a new organization at Stanford. Sophie’s current role in Synapse involves organizing gatherings to help bring individuals with brain injury together for both social events and guest speakers that can be enjoyed with families and student buddies. In addition to her interests in neuroscience, Sophie is involved in research at the Stanford Cardiovascular Institute and plans to pursue an MD/PhD after graduation.

Divya Gopisetty ('18)

Vice President of Outreach

Divya is a third-year undergraduate studying Human Biology with a focus on Pediatric Public Health. Within Synapse, she works to reach more individuals with brain injury throughout the Bay Area. Her favorite part about Synapse is how peer-to-peer interaction naturally supports emotional well-being. After graduation, she hopes to pursue an MD and specialize in Pediatric Endocrinology.

Claire Woodrow ('18)

Vice President of Buddy Program

Claire Woodrow is a junior undergraduate student pursuing a degree in Human Biology with a concentration in Neuroscience. After being introduced to patients suffering from brain trauma during high school, her desire for continued involvement with the brain injury community led her to join Stanford Synapse in its early stages during her freshman year. As initiator and organizer of the Buddy Program, Claire is inspired by the meaningful friendships Synapse helps to facilitate between individuals with brain injury and undergraduate students. Her personal experiences with her brain injury buddy constantly show her what a great impact Synapse has on those it serves. Claire is also a founding member of Synapse National, an organization which seeks to expand student support for the brain injury community to other universities across the country. In the future, Claire plans to continue studying the brain and hopefully become a physician.

Medical Student Liaisons

Alissa Totman

Alissa Totman is an MD/MPH candidate at the Stanford School of Medicine and the Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health. As an undergraduate she helped lead a student group called the MIT BrainTrust, which provides social support for individuals with brain injury. In fall of 2014 she joined forces with Jaclyn Konopka, fellow BrainTrust alumna and Stanford medical student, to start Stanford Synapse. Then in 2016, she created Synapse National, which provide undergraduates at universities across the US with leadership opportunities expanding social support for individuals with brain injury. After completing her MD/MPH she plans to pursue residency training in Physical Medicine and Rehabilitation and to further specialize in Traumatic Brain Injury.
